Great question! When describing a character comprehensively, here are dimensions people often explore:

## Physical & Sensory
- **Appearance** - build, facial features, distinctive marks
- **Mannerisms** - how they move, posture, nervous habits
- **Voice** - tone, accent, speech patterns, vocabulary choices
- **Sensory details** - what they smell like, textures they prefer, how they feel

## Psychological & Emotional
- **Emotional range** - what emotions they feel most, how intensely
- **Emotional triggers** - what sets them off, what comforts them
- **Internal conflicts** - contradictions between what they want and what they believe
- **Defensive mechanisms** - how they protect themselves emotionally
- **Vulnerabilities** - what genuinely hurts them

## Motivations & Desires
- **Core wants** - what drives them at the deepest level
- **Surface goals** - what they think they want
- **Fears** - what they're actually afraid of (often opposite of desires)
- **Secrets** - what they hide, from others and themselves

## Relationships & Social
- **Relationship patterns** - how they typically interact with people
- **Communication style** - direct, evasive, manipulative, honest, etc.
- **Loyalty & trust** - who they trust, how they show loyalty
- **Social role** - how others perceive them vs. who they are

## Complexity & Depth
- **Flaws & weaknesses** - what makes them fallible, interesting
- **Quirks & habits** - idiosyncratic behaviors and preferences
- **Growth arc** - how they've changed, how they could change
- **Contradictions** - the interesting tensions in their character

## Context & Worldview
- **Beliefs & values** - what they stand for, what's non-negotiable
- **Humor style** - how they use humor, what makes them laugh
- **Relationship to power, success, love, failure** - their philosophy
- **What they notice** - what details grab their attention (reveals priorities)
